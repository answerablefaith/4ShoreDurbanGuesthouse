Where to stay near Anstey’s Beach

Updated July 2026 · 4Shore Guesthouse

A quieter beach stay starts with simple choices: how close you are to the sand, where you park, and whether the room suits the way you travel.

On warm Durban mornings, Anstey’s Beach has an easy rhythm. The light comes up over the Indian Ocean, the rocks hold small pools, and the first walkers reach the sand before the day gets busy.

That is why where you stay matters. A room near the beach is not just about the view. It changes the shape of the day.

How to choose a stay near Anstey’s Beach

Start with the practical details. They are the things guests notice most once bags, towels, children, surfboards, or work laptops are involved.

  • Beach access: choose somewhere that makes the beach genuinely easy, not just nearby on a map.
  • Secure parking: useful for road trips, family stays, business visits, and guests coming and going during the day.
  • Room setup: check bed layout, guest count, private entrance, and whether the room fits a short or longer stay.
  • Direct contact: a quick conversation with the host can clear up rates, dates, and room choice.
At 4Shore: guests stay across Foreshore Drive from Anstey’s Beach, with nine en-suite rooms, private entrances, secure parking, Wi-Fi, air-conditioning, and direct enquiry booking.

What to expect from this part of the Bluff

The Bluff suits travellers who want the coast close by without staying in Durban’s busiest hotel areas. It works well for beach weekends, family visits, surf trips, business overnights, and guests who like a quieter base after a full day out.

You can start with a beach walk, head back for breakfast or a shower, then plan the rest of the day without fuss.

When a small guesthouse makes sense

If you want a large resort feel, this may not be the right style of stay. If you want owner-run accommodation, simple access, and direct help with dates, a smaller guesthouse can be easier.

It is especially useful when you want to ask a real person which room fits your stay.

Before you book, check these five things

  • Your dates and whether they fall over a busy weekend or school holiday.
  • Your final guest count and preferred bed setup.
  • Whether you need ground-floor access or easier luggage movement.
  • Parking arrangements if you are driving.
  • How quickly the host can confirm your final rate.

A good beach stay should feel easy before you arrive. Once the basics are clear, you can leave more room for the sea, the weather, and slow mornings across the road.
