Where to stay near Anstey’s Beach

Updated July 2026 · 4Shore Guesthouse

Choose a stay that keeps the beach simple

If you are looking for accommodation near Anstey’s Beach, the biggest advantage is not only the view. It is the ability to wake up, cross the road, and be at the sand before the day has become complicated.

For many guests, Bluff works well because it gives you a quieter coastal base while still keeping Durban day trips within reach. You can spend the morning by the surf, return to your room, and then head out again without feeling like you are fighting the city all day.

What to check before booking

  • Walking distance: Look for a stay that makes the beach genuinely easy, not just technically nearby.
  • Parking: Secure on-site parking matters if you are arriving by car, carrying beach gear, or staying more than one night.
  • Room setup: Couples, solo travellers, families, and twin-sharing guests need different sleeping arrangements.
  • Direct contact: A host who confirms your rate and availability clearly can remove a lot of booking stress.
At 4Shore: guests stay across Foreshore Drive from Anstey’s Beach, with nine en-suite rooms, private entrances, secure parking, Wi-Fi, air-conditioning, and direct enquiry booking.

Who this area suits

Anstey’s Beach is a good fit for guests who want Durban’s coastline without staying in the busiest parts of the city. It suits beach weekends, family stays, surf trips, short business visits, and travellers who want a quieter room after a full day out.

If you want a large resort atmosphere, you may prefer a different style of stay. If you want a small, owner-run guesthouse where the beach is part of the routine, this side of Bluff makes sense.

Book with the practical details in mind

Before you choose a room, check your dates, guest count, bed setup, whether breakfast matters, and whether you need a ground-floor or upper-level room. A simple direct enquiry is often the easiest way to match the stay to the person travelling.
