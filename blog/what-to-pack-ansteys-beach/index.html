What to pack for Anstey’s Beach: a simple guest checklist

Published 20 July 2026 · Updated 20 July 2026 · 4Shore Guesthouse

What should you pack for Anstey’s Beach? Start with sun protection, drinking water, a towel, dry clothes, secure footwear and a small waterproof pouch. Add only what your group will use.

Quick answer: Pack broad-spectrum sunscreen, a wide-brimmed hat, drinking water, towels, dry clothes, secure shoes, a waterproof pouch and a light wind layer for Anstey’s Beach on the Bluff, Durban. Families should add snacks, spare clothes and simple shade. Check the official beach status before anyone swims.

A nearby base changes how you pack. Take the items that solve real problems: strong sun, thirsty children, wet clothes, slippery ground and a quick change in wind or surf.

What are the essentials for an Anstey’s Beach day?

Put sun protection and water at the top of the list. Take broad-spectrum sunscreen, a hat with good coverage, drinking water and sunglasses. The Cancer Association of South Africa advises applying SPF 30 or SPF 50 sunscreen to children 20 to 30 minutes before outdoor activity, then reapplying every two hours and after swimming.

Carry one reusable water bottle per person if that suits your group. Add a small snack for a longer visit. Take one towel per swimmer and one dry change of clothes for anyone likely to get wet. A light top is useful after a swim and on cooler winter mornings.

What should go in the waterproof pouch?

Use a zipped waterproof pouch or sealed bag for phones, keys, cards and any medicine that must stay dry. Put wet swimwear in a separate bag so it does not soak clean clothes on the walk back.

Carry less rather than leaving a large bag unattended. Leave valuables at 4Shore Guesthouse where possible. Secure on-site parking is useful for arrival and luggage, but bags and electronics should not be displayed in a parked vehicle.

Agree on a meeting point before the group spreads out, and store Winston’s number, +27 83 254 4825, for guesthouse and local questions.

Which shoes work best at Anstey’s Beach?

Secure sandals or slip-on shoes are fine for the sand and boardwalk. Choose firmer shoes with grip if you plan to walk near rocks, wet steps or rough ground. Bare feet can be uncomfortable on hot sand and are a poor choice around sharp shells or slippery surfaces.

Pack shoes for the activity. Ask on arrival if an area is open and suitable before stepping onto wet surfaces.

What should families pack for children?

Families need a little more, but the bag should still be easy to carry. Add spare clothes, extra water, simple snacks, sun hats and a shade option that one adult can manage. Choose one small activity for rest breaks, such as a book, cards or a bucket.

Before reaching the sand, name the adult responsible for each child near the water. Take a current photo of children in their beach clothes and choose an easy meeting point, such as the lifeguard station. Bright clothing is easier to spot in a busy area.

Do you need different items in winter or summer?

Durban’s coast can feel warm in winter sun, then cooler when cloud or wind moves in. Pack a light long-sleeved top or wind layer from May to August. For a late-afternoon visit, add a dry layer before the temperature drops.

In warmer months, shade and drinking water matter even more. CANSA advises using clothing, hats, shade and sunscreen together. Check the forecast before leaving, then look at the sea and sky when you arrive. The conditions in front of you decide whether the plan still makes sense.

What should you check before packing swimwear?

Check the official eThekwini Beach Water Quality website on the day of your visit. It provides water-quality information, beach facilities and lifeguard schedule details. Use the newest dated information, not an old screenshot or message.

At the beach, find the marked bathing area and ask the on-duty lifeguard where swimming is allowed. In 2026, eThekwini Municipality again warned visitors to swim only at designated bathing beaches while lifeguards are on duty and to keep children under constant adult supervision.

If the area is closed, no lifeguard is on duty or the surf looks rough, keep the swimwear dry. Take a safe walk, enjoy the view or switch plans. Our Anstey’s Beach safety guide explains the checks in more detail.

FAQ: what to pack for Anstey’s Beach

What should I pack for Anstey’s Beach?

Pack sunscreen, a hat, drinking water, towels, dry clothes, secure shoes, a waterproof pouch and a light layer. Check the current beach status before adding swimming to the plan.

What sunscreen should children use at Anstey’s Beach?

CANSA advises broad-spectrum SPF 30 or SPF 50 sunscreen for children. Apply it 20 to 30 minutes before outdoor activity, then reapply every two hours and after swimming.

Do I need special shoes for Anstey’s Beach?

No special shoes are needed for a basic sand visit. Secure sandals work for the beach, while firmer shoes with grip are better near rocks, wet steps or rough ground.

What should families add to the beach bag?

Families should add spare clothes, extra water, simple snacks, hats, manageable shade and one small activity for breaks. Keep the bag light enough for one adult to carry while supervising children.

Should I check Anstey’s Beach conditions before leaving?

Yes. Check the official eThekwini beach information, then confirm the designated bathing area and lifeguard cover when you arrive. Never enter a closed or unsupervised swimming area.
