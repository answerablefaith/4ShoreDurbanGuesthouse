Beach-day checklist

What to pack for Anstey’s Beach: a simple guest checklist

Published 20 July 2026 · 4Shore Guesthouse

Pack light, but cover the basics: sun care, water, a dry change of clothes and a plan for changing beach conditions.

Quick answer: Take sunscreen, a hat, drinking water, towels, suitable shoes, a dry bag and a light layer. Families should add spare clothes, snacks and simple shade. Before swimming, check current beach information and use a designated bathing area while lifeguards are on duty.

A good beach bag makes the day easier. You don’t need to carry everything you own. You only need the items that protect you from the sun, keep valuables dry and help you change plans when the wind or surf shifts.

4Shore Guesthouse is across Foreshore Drive from Anstey’s Beach. That makes it easier to return for a forgotten item or a dry change of clothes than it would be from a distant Durban base.

Start with sun and water

Durban sun can feel strong even on a mild day. Pack sunscreen, a hat and enough drinking water for your group. Apply sunscreen before you leave, then take it with you for later use.

A small reusable bottle for each person is often easier than one large bottle. Add a simple snack if you expect to stay for more than a short walk.

Keep clothes and valuables dry

Take one towel per person and a dry change of clothes. A waterproof pouch or sealed bag is useful for phones, keys and wallets. Keep wet swimwear in a separate bag for the return trip.

Secure sandals work well on sand. Closed or firmer shoes are a better choice if you plan to walk near rocks, wet paths or rough ground.

Pack a little extra for children

Families should add spare clothes, extra water, simple snacks and a small shade option. One light activity can help during a break from the sun.

Keep the bag manageable. A child who is tired, wet or hungry usually needs a quick reset more than another beach toy.

Add one layer for wind or cooler weather

Beach weather can change during the day. A light top or wind layer helps after a swim and on cooler winter mornings. It also makes an early walk or late-afternoon visit more comfortable.

Check the forecast before leaving, but look at the sea and sky as well. A bright start does not always mean the whole day will stay still and warm.

Check conditions before swimming

Beach status can change. Use current eThekwini information and signs at the beach. Swim only in a designated bathing area while lifeguards are on duty. Keep children under close adult supervision.

If the water looks rough or the bathing area is not clear, switch to a walk, a view stop or another plan. Read our Anstey’s Beach safety guide for a fuller check before entering the water.

FAQ: packing for Anstey’s Beach

What should I take to Anstey’s Beach?

Take water, sunscreen, a hat, towels, suitable footwear, a dry pouch and a light layer.

Do I need special shoes?

No special shoes are required for a simple beach visit. Secure sandals suit the sand. Firmer shoes help near rocks or wet paths.

What should families add?

Add spare clothes, extra water, snacks, simple shade and one small activity for rest breaks.

Should I check beach conditions first?

Yes. Check current municipal information and swim only where bathing is allowed and lifeguards are on duty.
